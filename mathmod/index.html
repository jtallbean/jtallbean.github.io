Learning engineering by building robots and destroying them.

Founding

In April 2019, along with three other friends, we founded the combat robotics team at Duke University. The goal of the club is to engage students in engineering through competition and teach out to build a robot from the ground up.

CAD/Simulation

CAD tools such as Fusion 360 and Solidworks are utilized throughout the entire design process. The goal of combat robotics is to minimize weight while maximizing damage potential and durability.

The CAD portion involves designing with production in mind, simplifying toolpaths, and avoiding undercuts and other complex geometries.

FEA simulation as an example is used to calculate the forces imparted on a spinning blade/lever arm. At the forces involved here potentially up to thousands of Newtons, FEA is essential to strengthening key areas and saving weight in others.

Electronics

The wiring harnesses for each robot are essential to the control and operation. A controller drives an electronic speed controller to each motor, dictating its speed.

In some robots, PID control is used to precisely control the speed of a spinning blade when it makes contact. A motor that continually drives a stuck blade may burn itself out, so PID is used to detect these situations and control voltage accordingly.
